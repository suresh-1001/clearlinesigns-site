Corporate Sign Programs

What a Complete Corporate
Sign Program Looks Like

A single lobby sign is a starting point. A complete corporate sign program is something different — it's every touchpoint a visitor, employee, or candidate encounters from the parking structure to the boardroom. That includes the lobby reception display, elevator wraps on every floor, directional signs in corridors, department identification, environmental graphics in collaboration spaces, exterior building identification, and branded conference room signage throughout. When it's done right, it's invisible — the space simply feels like a coherent expression of the company.

Silicon Valley companies face a specific challenge with signage: your brand is evolving constantly. A startup that was 40 people two years ago is now 400, across two buildings, with a rebrand in progress. The sign program has to keep pace — which means a sign partner who can execute a full lobby installation and then update wayfinding across three floors a year later without treating it as a completely new project.

We've built sign programs for funded startups moving into their first real office and for enterprise-scale campuses requiring coordinated installs across multiple floors and buildings. Every project starts with the same question: what does this company actually need, from the parking lot to the CEO's office, to make the brand feel fully realized?

How It Works

From Sign Audit to
Fully Branded Campus

Corporate projects require a structured process. Here's how we manage a full sign program from scope to installation.

1
Step One

Sign Audit & Scope

We walk your building with your facilities or marketing lead, photograph every existing sign, and produce a complete scope document identifying what stays, what replaces, and what's missing. For new buildouts, we work from architectural drawings and your floor plan to build the sign schedule before construction is complete.

2
Step Two

Design & Brand Review

Every sign type in the program is designed to your brand standards and submitted for approval. For corporate clients with a brand team, we submit a complete design deck — materials, finishes, dimensions, placement, and renderings on photos of your actual space. No approving something you've never seen in context.

3
Step Three

Fabrication & Permitting

Interior signs go into production immediately after approval. Exterior signs and anything requiring a City of San Jose permit go through the permit process in parallel with interior fabrication — so both are ready around the same time. We manage the permit timeline and communicate proactively if anything shifts.

4
Step Four

Phased Installation

Installation is scheduled in phases around your operations — typically lobby and reception first, then floor-by-floor interior signs, then exterior. For elevator wraps, we coordinate access with your building management and install during off-hours or weekend windows. Every phase is signed off before the next begins.
