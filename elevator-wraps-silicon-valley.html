About Elevator Wraps

What Are Elevator Wraps
and Why Do They Work?

An elevator wrap is full-color printed vinyl applied directly to elevator doors, interior cab walls, ceiling panels, or floor-level kick plates. The result is a surface that looks nothing like a standard elevator — it can appear to be an ornate carved door, a branded product display, a lifestyle scene, or a photorealistic environment. Every person who uses that elevator sees the graphic, multiple times per day, from a distance of about 18 inches.

In a commercial building, elevator doors are some of the highest-impression real estate that most companies completely ignore. A single well-executed elevator wrap gets seen by every employee, visitor, and vendor who passes through the building — often more frequently than lobby signage. For corporate campuses with multiple elevator banks, a coordinated multi-elevator program creates an entire environmental graphics experience that makes an ordinary building feel intentional and branded throughout.

The vinyl used for elevator wraps is engineered for high-contact, high-traffic surfaces — scuff-resistant, easy to clean, and rated for years of daily use in commercial environments. Installation is coordinated with building management to minimize disruption, typically done during off-hours or on weekends in occupied buildings.

We've executed a multi-phase elevator wrap program for Intuit's Mountain View campus across two project cycles, a full cab interior for Pathwater, and a complete office door wrap program for Ring (Amazon). These aren't single-elevator installs — they're coordinated programs requiring precise templating, phased installation, and multi-stakeholder approval in occupied corporate buildings.

Seam Alignment — The Critical Detail

Elevator doors have a gap between panels when closed and misalign when opening. Every elevator is different — door gap widths, panel heights, hardware protrusions, and frame offsets all vary. We template each elevator individually before production so graphics align precisely when the doors are closed and read cleanly from a distance. A graphic that doesn't align at the seam looks worse than no graphic at all.

Building Schedule Coordination

Elevator wraps in occupied buildings require coordination with property management for elevator downtime, access scheduling, and contractor approval. We handle all of this as part of the project. Most elevator door installations are completed during off-hours or weekends — interior cab wraps require a scheduled maintenance window. We manage the logistics so your facilities team doesn't have to.

Commercial-Grade Vinyl for High-Traffic Surfaces

Elevator doors and cab interiors are high-contact surfaces — hands, bags, carts, and cleaning equipment make contact daily. We use scuff-resistant, laminated commercial-grade cast vinyl rated for high-touch applications. The laminate layer protects the graphic from cleaning chemicals and physical wear. Your wrap looks as sharp in year two as it did on install day.

How It Works

From Concept to
Installed Elevator Wrap

Elevator wrap projects require more preparation than standard vinyl installations. Here's the complete process.

1
Step One

Site Survey & Templating

We visit your building, photograph each elevator, measure door panel dimensions exactly, and note all hardware protrusions, frame offsets, and door gap widths. Every elevator is templated individually — this step determines how the final printed panels are produced and ensures seam alignment when installed.

2
Step Two

Concept Design & Proof

We produce a design proof showing your graphic positioned on a photo of your actual elevator, to scale. For multi-elevator programs, all concepts are presented together so you can see how the full bank reads as a complete installation. Revisions included before production starts.

3
Step Three

Production

Graphics are printed on commercial-grade scuff-resistant laminated vinyl, panel by panel, sized to the exact measurements from the site survey. Quality-checked against the template before we schedule installation to catch any production discrepancies before they become on-site problems.

4
Step Four

Coordinated Installation

Installation is scheduled with building management — typically off-hours or weekends for door wraps, and a maintenance window for interior cab wraps. We clean the surface, apply the panels with precise alignment, trim edges cleanly, and confirm seam alignment before signing off. Template files are retained for future refresh cycles.
