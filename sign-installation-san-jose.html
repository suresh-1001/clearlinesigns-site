About Sign Installation

What Sign Installation
Actually Involves

Sign installation is a trade skill, not a delivery task. Exterior building signs require structural anchors rated for wind load, electrical conduit runs for illuminated signs, and lifts or scaffolding for signs mounted above the first floor. Monument signs require concrete footings poured to spec. Post-and-panel signs need auger-drilled posts set in concrete at the correct depth for California soil conditions. Getting these wrong means a sign that shifts, leaks, or fails inspection — or worse, comes down in a storm.

Interior installation has its own requirements. Heavy dimensional letter sets need wall blocking or direct anchoring into studs at precise spacing. Lobby signs on glass or finished drywall require careful surface prep and the right adhesive system for the substrate. ADA signs must be mounted at the exact height and lateral placement specified by California Title 24 — not close, exactly right. We survey every location before fabrication and produce a sign that mounts correctly the first time.

For permitted exterior signs in San Jose, installation can only happen after the permit is issued and the job card is posted. We coordinate the timing so fabrication finishes as close to permit approval as possible — keeping your project moving without sitting on a finished sign waiting for city paperwork. After installation, we schedule the inspection and handle any field corrections on the spot.

Channel Letter Installation

Individual letter mounting to building fascia or raceway — including electrical conduit runs, transformer mounting, and GFI connections for illuminated signs. We handle the electrical rough-in and coordinate with your electrician of record where required by city permit.

Illuminated · Electrical · Raceway

Monument Sign Installation

Concrete footer poured to depth based on sign height and wind load calculations. Steel anchor bolt patterns set to spec before the pour. Sign cabinet or face set level and plumb, with electrical conduit stubbed up inside the footer for illuminated monument signs.

Concrete Footings · Structural

Building & Fascia Sign Installation

Through-wall anchoring with toggle bolts, expansion anchors, or structural adhesive depending on the wall substrate. For large dimensional letter sets, we core-drill and install backing plates where direct stud attachment isn't possible. Every anchor point load-rated before install.

Dimensional · Fascia · Wall Mount

Post & Panel Installation

Auger-drilled posts set in concrete at the correct depth for California soil conditions and sign height. Double-post systems aligned to a string line for exact spacing. Panel attachment using carriage bolts or welded brackets depending on panel material and wind exposure zone.

Concrete · Double-Post · Aligned

Lobby & Interior Sign Installation

Dimensional letter sets, acrylic panels, and wall-mounted lobby signs on drywall, glass, tile, stone, and wood. We locate studs, set anchors, and use spacer standoffs for floating letter effects — all leveled to a laser line. ADA signs mounted to California Title 24 height and placement specifications.

Lobby · ADA · Dimensional Letters

Vinyl & Graphic Installation

Window graphics, wall murals, floor decals, and large-format printed vinyl applied to commercial surfaces. Wet or dry application method matched to material and surface. Surface prep, application, and final inspection — no bubbles, no lifting edges, no misaligned panels.

How It Works

From First Call to
Sign Installed & Inspected

A clear process built around the real constraints of sign installation in San Jose — permits, landlords, inspections, and all.

1
Step One

Site Survey & Scope

We visit the location to assess the mounting surface, measure the sign area, check for electrical access, identify any structural considerations, and review permit requirements. For interior installs, we assess wall substrate and stud placement. No assumptions — every install method is confirmed on site before design starts.

2
Step Two

Design, Proof & Permit

We produce scaled design proofs showing your sign on the actual building or wall. For permitted exterior signs, we prepare and submit the full permit package to the City of San Jose — elevation drawings, structural details, and electrical calculations. Fabrication starts once design is approved.

3
Step Three

Fabrication

Every sign is built in-house to the exact dimensions and specifications confirmed during the site survey. Anchor hole patterns, standoff spacing, and electrical knockout positions are all fabricated to match the install — not adjusted in the field. For permitted signs, fabrication is timed to permit approval to minimize holding time.

4
Step Four

Installation & Inspection

Our crew handles the complete installation — rigging, anchoring, electrical connections, leveling, and cleanup. For permitted signs, we post the job card and schedule the city inspection. We're present at the inspection and handle any required corrections on the spot. You get a sign that's installed correctly, inspected, and closed out.
