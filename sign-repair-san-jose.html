About Sign Repair

Sign Repair, Refacing, and Refurbishing
— What We Fix and How

Most "broken" signs in San Jose don't need to be replaced. A cracked cabinet face, sun-faded graphics, peeling vinyl, or a sign that still shows the previous tenant's brand — these are repair and refacing jobs, at a fraction of new-sign cost. We replace acrylic and polycarbonate faces, re-letter and re-wrap existing structures, repaint weathered dimensional letters and cabinets, and swap out damaged panels on monument and post-and-panel signs.

Refacing is the one most building owners don't know to ask for: the existing sign structure — cabinet, frame, mounting — stays put, and only the visible faces and graphics are replaced with your current branding. For rebrands, new tenants taking over a space, or franchises updating to current brand standards, refacing reuses the permitted structure and typically avoids a new sign permit entirely.

We're straightforward about scope. Structural, face, vinyl, paint, and hardware work we handle directly. For electrical and LED component service inside illuminated signs, we coordinate licensed sign electricians we work with regularly — one quote, one point of contact, no runaround. And if a sign is genuinely past saving, we'll tell you that too, and quote the replacement honestly.
