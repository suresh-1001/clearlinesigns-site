About Blade Signs

What Are Blade Signs
and When Do You Need One

A blade sign — also called a projecting sign or hanging sign — extends perpendicular from a building facade rather than lying flat against it. That 90-degree orientation makes it readable to foot traffic moving along the sidewalk, not just pedestrians walking directly toward your entrance. On a busy retail corridor or medical office strip in San Jose, that difference in visibility can be significant.

Blade signs are common in downtown San Jose, Santana Row, and Willow Glen, where buildings sit close to the sidewalk and pedestrian traffic moves parallel to storefronts. They're also widely used inside buildings — suspended from ceilings in hospitals, shopping centers, and office lobbies as overhead directional signs.

San Jose requires a sign permit for most exterior projecting signs, including dimensional requirements that limit how far the sign can extend from the building face and its minimum clearance above the sidewalk. We handle the permit submittal through SJPermits.org and coordinate landlord approval packages for tenant applications.

Sign Options

Blade Sign Types
We Design & Install

Double-Sided Exterior Blade

The most common projecting sign format — visible in both directions from a single mounting bracket. Aluminum frame with printed or dimensional face panels. Available illuminated or non-lit.

Illuminated Projecting Sign

Cabinet-style blade sign with LED-lit translucent face panels for 24/7 visibility. Common for restaurants and retail in evening foot traffic corridors. Requires electrical connection and sign permit.

Acrylic & Metal Blade Signs

Flat-panel projecting signs in acrylic or aluminum — clean and modern for professional services, medical offices, and boutique retail where the aesthetic needs to match an upscale environment.

Ceiling-Hung Interior Signs

Suspended directional and identification signs for hospital corridors, shopping center interiors, airport-style wayfinding, and large commercial lobbies. Single or double-sided, rigid or fabric.

Bracket-Mounted Signs

Decorative wrought iron or powder-coated steel brackets with a sign panel — common for boutique retail, wine bars, and professional services where the mounting hardware itself contributes to the storefront aesthetic.

Flagpole & Banner Arm Signs

Vertical banner holders and projecting flag-format signs for commercial buildings and retail centers. Fabric or rigid panel options — easy to swap for seasonal promotions or tenant changes.
