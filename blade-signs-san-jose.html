About Blade Signs

What Are Blade Signs
and Why Do Storefronts Use Them?

A blade sign — also called a projecting sign or hanging sign — mounts perpendicular to the building face and extends out over the sidewalk. Unlike a flat fascia sign that reads only from directly in front, a blade sign is visible to foot traffic approaching from either direction. For businesses in dense downtown corridors, mixed-use retail strips, or anywhere pedestrians are the primary customer, that 90-degree orientation is the difference between being seen and being missed.

Blade signs are typically double-sided and constructed from aluminum cabinet frames with acrylic faces, flat aluminum panels, or wooden substrates depending on the building aesthetic. They can be internally illuminated with LED for nighttime visibility, or finished as non-illuminated signs with vinyl or painted graphics. Bracket hardware mounts directly to the building fascia or a wall-mounted arm above the entrance.

In San Jose, projecting signs require a sign permit from the City's Development Services Department, and depending on the building, landlord approval as well. We handle both — permit submittal, landlord approval package, fabrication, and installation — so you don't have to manage multiple vendors or the permitting process yourself.

Sign Options

Types of Blade Signs
We Design & Install

Illuminated Cabinet Blade Sign

Aluminum cabinet with translucent acrylic face panels, internally lit with LED. Bright, even illumination visible day and night. The most common choice for retail storefronts and restaurants that operate into evening hours.

Flat Aluminum Blade Sign

Non-illuminated flat aluminum panel with routed or applied graphics. Clean, modern look suited to professional offices, boutiques, and buildings with architectural restrictions on illuminated signage.

Acrylic Face Blade Sign

Aluminum frame with digitally printed or vinyl-applied acrylic faces. Durable, weather-resistant, and available in any shape — rectangular, arched, or custom profile to match the building aesthetic.

Wood & Metal Blade Sign

HDU foam or natural wood substrate with metal hardware and bracket — suited to restaurants, cafes, boutiques, and historic district buildings where a warmer, handcrafted aesthetic fits the brand.

Halo-Lit Blade Sign

Dimensional letters or logo mounted on a blade panel with LED backlighting for a halo effect at night. Upscale look for restaurants, hotels, and premium retail — visible and distinctive after dark.

Icon-Only Blade Sign

A graphic or icon shape — scissor, coffee cup, medical cross — rather than a text panel. Fast visual shorthand for the type of business, readable at a glance by pedestrians moving past the storefront.
