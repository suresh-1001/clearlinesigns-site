About Cabinet Signs

What Are Cabinet Signs
and When Do You Need One?

A cabinet sign — also called a box sign or lightbox — is an aluminum cabinet with an illuminated translucent face panel that displays your business name, logo, and graphics. The face is typically made from acrylic or polycarbonate, printed with vinyl graphics or directly with UV-curable ink, and backlit with LEDs for even, consistent illumination day and night.

Cabinet signs are the standard choice for strip mall and shopping center tenants in San Jose. Most commercial landlords in centers like Westfield Valley Fair, Eastridge Mall, and along El Camino Real specify cabinet sign criteria in their lease sign packages — a set size, cabinet depth, and illumination type that all tenants must match. We know how to design within those criteria and manage the landlord approval process alongside the City of San Jose sign permit submittal.

Beyond shopping centers, cabinet signs work well for gas stations, drive-throughs, auto service businesses, medical offices, and any location where a consistent, branded backlit display outperforms individual channel letters. They're also a cost-effective option when you need double-sided visibility — projecting blade cabinet signs illuminate your brand from both directions for foot and vehicle traffic.

Sign Options

Types of Cabinet Signs
We Design & Install

Single-Face Cabinet Signs

Flush-mounted to building fascia with one illuminated face panel. The most common format for strip mall tenants — sized to match landlord sign criteria and mounted at the standard fascia height.

Double-Face Projecting Cabinets

Two-sided illuminated box signs that extend perpendicular from the building — visible to pedestrians and drivers approaching from either direction. Common on street-facing retail corridors and downtown San Jose storefronts.

LED Lightbox Displays

Ultra-thin LED cabinet signs with edge-lit or backlit panels — a cleaner, lower-profile alternative to traditional cabinet depth for modern storefronts and interior lightbox applications.

Flex-Face Cabinet Signs

Large-format cabinets with flexible translucent face material instead of rigid acrylic — the standard for pylon sign faces, monument sign inserts, and large shopping center displays that span more than 8 feet.

Non-Illuminated Cabinet Signs

Aluminum cabinet signs with opaque printed faces for locations where illumination isn't required or permitted. A cost-effective option for interior directories, suite identification, and directional sign programs.

Drive-Through & Menu Board Cabinets

Illuminated menu board cabinets and drive-through identification signs for restaurants, coffee shops, pharmacies, and any business with a drive-up service lane.
