About Indoor Signs

What Indoor Signs Cover
and Why It Matters

Interior signage is everything visitors, employees, and clients encounter once they're inside your building — from the lobby sign at reception to the room ID plaques on every door, the wayfinding arrows in the hallways, and the brand mural on the main office wall. Most businesses treat these as separate purchases from separate vendors. We handle all of it as a coordinated sign program, so materials, finishes, and typestyles stay consistent throughout the space.

In California, a significant portion of interior signage is governed by ADA compliance requirements under Title 24. Room identification signs — including restroom signs, suite numbers, conference room plaques, and exit signs — must meet specific requirements for braille, tactile characters, mounting height, and contrast. Non-compliant interior signage creates legal exposure. We specify and produce ADA-compliant signs for every applicable location and handle the documentation if required.

Material selection for interior signs runs a different set of trade-offs than exterior work. UV resistance matters less; brand accuracy, tactile finish, and dimensional depth matter more. We work in acrylic, brushed aluminum, PVC, dibond, frosted vinyl, and digitally printed substrates — choosing based on what each sign type and location actually needs, not just what's easiest to produce.

How It Works

From Space Walkthrough
to Signs on the Wall

Interior sign programs involve more locations and more sign types than a single exterior sign. Here's how we keep it organized.

1
Step One

Space Walkthrough & Sign Schedule

We walk your space — or review your floor plan if you're pre-occupancy — and produce a sign schedule: every location, sign type, size, and ADA requirement mapped out before design starts. No surprises mid-project when we realize a sign was missed.

2
Step Two

Design & Proof

We produce design proofs for every sign type — showing materials, finishes, typography, and placement on your actual walls. All sign types are designed together so finishes and scales are consistent. You approve before anything goes into production.

3
Step Three

Fabrication

All sign types produced in-house — acrylic cutting and finishing, aluminum fabrication, digital printing, and vinyl production. ADA signs are produced to California Title 24 specifications including braille translation. Most interior sign programs are ready in 2–3 weeks.

4
Step Four

Installation

Our crew installs everything in a single scheduled visit where possible — lobby signs, room IDs, wall graphics, and window film all in one day. We work around your business hours and coordinate with facilities or building management as needed.
