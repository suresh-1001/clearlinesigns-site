About Pylon Signs

What Are Pylon Signs
and When Do You Need One?

Pylon signs — also called pole signs — are freestanding exterior signs elevated on one or two poles above grade, typically positioned at the street edge of a property for maximum roadside visibility. Unlike monument signs, which sit at or near ground level, pylon signs are designed to be seen from a distance and at speed — by drivers on a 45 mph arterial road, from an intersection, or from a freeway off-ramp. They're most common for gas stations, fast food locations, auto dealerships, strip mall anchors, and hotels where being visible from hundreds of feet away is the primary objective.

In San Jose, pylon signs are subject to some of the most complex permitting of any sign type. Height, setback, sign area, illumination, and the number of signs per parcel are all regulated by the City's sign ordinance — and they vary significantly by zoning district. A commercial corridor like Monterey Road has different allowances than an industrial zone on North First Street or a freeway-adjacent parcel near I-280. We review the applicable sign ordinance for your specific parcel before quoting, so the design we propose is actually permittable — not a concept that gets rejected after fabrication begins.

Structural engineering is often required for pylon signs over a certain height or sign area. We coordinate the engineering, prepare permit drawings, and submit through SJPermits.org. Electrical permits are required separately for illuminated pylon signs, and a licensed electrician must make the final connection — we coordinate that too.

Pylon Sign Options

Types of Pylon Signs
We Design & Install

Single-Tenant Pylon Signs

A freestanding pole sign displaying one business — common for gas stations, fast food, hotels, and standalone retail buildings where the business needs roadside visibility from a distance or at speed.

Multi-Tenant Pylon Signs

Pole signs with stacked tenant panels — each tenant gets a panel on a shared structure. Common for strip centers and multi-tenant commercial properties where multiple businesses need roadside visibility from a single sign location.

Illuminated Pylon Signs

Cabinet-faced pylon signs with internally illuminated LED panels. Standard for most commercial pylon applications — the illuminated face is visible in daylight and at night, and LED lighting is now the standard for new installations.

Non-Illuminated Pole Signs

Non-lit pylon signs for locations where illumination is not permitted or not needed. Typically use aluminum panels, dimensional letters, or painted substrates. Lower cost and simpler permitting — no electrical permit required.

LED Message Center Pylons

Pylon signs with programmable LED electronic message center panels — allowing the business to change pricing, specials, and messaging remotely. Subject to San Jose EMC regulations on animation and brightness.

Pylon Sign Refacing & Retrofits

Replacing the face panels or cabinet on an existing pylon structure — a cost-effective way to rebrand or update a pole sign without replacing the pole and foundation. Typically requires only a sign permit, not a structural permit.
