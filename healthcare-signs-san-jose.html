Healthcare Signage

What Healthcare Facilities Need
from Their Signage

Healthcare signage operates under requirements that most other commercial environments don't face. Every permanent room in a medical facility — exam rooms, procedure suites, restrooms, stairwells, and staff areas — requires an ADA and California Title 24 compliant tactile sign. Exterior building signs require permits. Wayfinding has to work for patients who may be stressed, in pain, or unfamiliar with the facility layout. And all of it has to look professional enough to reinforce patient confidence in the practice.

We work with the full range of healthcare environments in Silicon Valley — single-location medical and dental practices, multi-location urgent care chains, hospital campuses, outpatient surgery centers, rehabilitation facilities, and medical office buildings with multiple tenant suites. The sign package required for each is different, and we build the right scope for each facility type rather than applying a one-size-fits-all template.

For new build-outs and tenant improvements, we work directly with general contractors and interior architects — producing sign schedules, fabricating compliant signs, and coordinating installation to meet Certificate of Occupancy requirements. For existing facilities coming into compliance or refreshing their signage, we start with an audit and quote the full package from there.

How It Works

From Compliance Audit to
Installed Healthcare Sign Package

Healthcare sign projects vary in scope — a single-practice ADA package can be completed in 2 weeks; a full hospital campus wayfinding program takes longer. We scope accurately upfront so you know what to expect.

1
Step One

Consultation & Compliance Audit

We review your facility layout, identify all sign requirements under ADA and California Title 24, and produce a complete sign schedule. For exterior signs, we check San Jose sign code requirements for your zoning district and building type before any design work begins.

2
Step Two

Design & Approvals

Interior sign designs are proofed for your review. Exterior sign designs go through the permit process — we prepare and submit to the City of San Jose and coordinate landlord approvals for leased spaces. You approve designs before fabrication starts.

3
Step Three

Fabrication

ADA signs are fabricated to California Title 24 specifications — tactile characters, Grade 2 Braille, non-glare finish, correct contrast. Exterior signs are built to commercial standards with UV-stable materials. Every sign is inspected before packaging and delivery.

4
Step Four

Installation & Documentation

We install at correct ADA mounting heights, coordinate with your facility schedule to minimize patient disruption, and provide complete installation documentation for your permit file. For TI projects, we work to your CO timeline and communicate directly with your GC.
