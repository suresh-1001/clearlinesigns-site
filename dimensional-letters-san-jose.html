About Dimensional Letters

What Are Dimensional Letters
and Why Do They Work?

Dimensional letters are three-dimensional individual characters — cut or fabricated from a solid material, finished, and mounted directly to a wall surface. Unlike a flat printed sign or a vinyl decal, dimensional letters have physical depth — typically ½" to 2" — which creates shadow lines that make the sign read more clearly at a distance and communicate a level of permanence and quality that flat graphics simply can't match.

For Silicon Valley tech companies, the lobby sign is often the first physical impression a customer, investor, or new hire gets of your brand. Dimensional letters in brushed metal, polished acrylic, or precision-painted foam signal that this is a company that takes its brand seriously. The Exnodes installation above — fabricated in brushed aluminum with a three-dimensional logo structure — is a good example of what's achievable when material, finish, and fabrication quality are all dialed in.

We fabricate dimensional signs in-house and through our fabrication partners, working from your existing logo files. No permit is required for interior lobby signs in California, which keeps the timeline clean — most dimensional letter projects are designed, fabricated, and installed within 3–5 weeks from approved proof.

Materials

Every Material We Fabricate
Dimensional Letters In

The right material depends on your wall surface, your brand aesthetic, your budget, and how the sign will be lit — or not lit. Here's a clear breakdown of every option.

Acrylic (Cast & Extruded)

The most versatile dimensional letter material. Clear, white, black, or custom color — available in flat cut, beveled edges, or with a polished face. Acrylic letters can be painted to any color, left in their natural translucent state, or used with LED backlighting for a halo effect. Clean, sharp, and durable.

  • Available in any color or finish
  • ½" to 1" standard depth
  • Flat-face or polished-edge options
  • Works with halo-lit LED behind
  • Best for: clean corporate, tech, medical

Metal (Aluminum, Stainless, Brass)

Flat-cut metal letters are CNC-routed from aluminum, stainless steel, or brass sheet. Finish options include brushed, polished, anodized, or powder-coated in any color. Metal letters communicate the highest level of permanence and quality — the material choice for executive reception areas, financial services, and flagship tech company lobbies. As seen in the Exnodes installation above.

  • Brushed, polished, or painted finish
  • Aluminum, stainless, or brass
  • Any thickness — typically ⅛" to ¼"
  • Halo-lit possible with aluminum
  • Best for: premium, executive, financial

PVC (Sintra)

Rigid PVC board, also known as Sintra, is a lightweight alternative to acrylic — easier to fabricate in complex shapes and paintable to any color. Slightly less premium feel than acrylic or metal, but cost-effective for larger letter sizes and complex custom logo cuts. Interior-only application.

  • Lightweight — easy to mount
  • Paintable to any color
  • ½" to ¾" standard depth
  • Cost-effective for large formats
  • Best for: budget-conscious, large logos

HDU Foam (High-Density Urethane)

High-density urethane foam routed to shape and painted — the best choice for very large-format dimensional logos and signs where weight is a concern. HDU is strong enough to take a painted finish that holds up indoors, light enough to mount on standard drywall without structural backing. Common for oversized dimensional installations like the Fujitsu and Cohere logos shown above.

  • Lightweight — mounts on any wall
  • Can achieve 1" to 4" depth
  • Paintable to any color
  • Ideal for large logo structures
  • Best for: large-format, 3D logo shapes

Painted Dimensional Composites

Some complex logo shapes — like the Fujitsu Solutions Lab sign above — are built from multiple materials in combination: foam for the base structure, acrylic for letter faces, metal accents. The final product is painted in your exact brand colors for a seamless, cohesive look that matches your logo at any scale.

  • Multi-material construction
  • Painted to exact brand colors
  • Any depth and complexity
  • Full color matching available
  • Best for: complex logo structures

Gemini Cast Aluminum Plaques

Cast aluminum plaques from Gemini Sign Products — our authorized reseller relationship gives us access to Gemini's full catalog of cast aluminum letters, plaques, and dimensional sign systems. Cast aluminum offers a distinct, permanent character that flat-cut metal can't replicate — the material of choice for institutional plaques, building directories, and permanent identification signs.

Mounting Options

Three Ways to Mount
Dimensional Letters

The mounting method affects how the sign looks on the wall — each has a distinct visual character and installation requirement.

Flush Mount

Letters mount flat against the wall surface — clean, minimal, and works on any wall type including textured surfaces. The simplest installation method and the lowest profile look. Common for acrylic and PVC letters in clean corporate interiors.

Standoff Mount

Letters are mounted on metal standoff pins — typically ¾" to 2" out from the wall. This creates a visible shadow behind each letter that changes with the ambient lighting, giving the sign a three-dimensional presence even when viewed straight-on. The most popular mounting style for dimensional letter signs in Silicon Valley offices.

Halo-Lit (Back-Lit)

LED modules are mounted behind the letters — facing the wall rather than forward. The light reflects off the wall and creates a glow around each letter. The letters themselves appear as solid material (typically brushed metal or acrylic) while the halo effect provides illumination. Creates the most premium visual impact, especially in dimmer lobby environments.

How It Works

From Logo File to
Installed Dimensional Sign

Most dimensional letter projects follow this process — typically 3–5 weeks from first conversation to installed sign.

1
Step One

Site Review & Consultation

We visit your San Jose or Silicon Valley office to assess the wall — surface type, structural backing, ambient lighting, and sight lines from the entrance. This is where we recommend material and mounting style based on what will actually look best in your specific space.

2
Step Two

Design & Scaled Mockup

We take your vector logo files, size the sign to the correct scale for your wall dimensions, and produce a photo-realistic mockup showing the sign on your actual wall. This step prevents surprises — you see exactly what you're approving before fabrication begins.

3
Step Three

Fabrication

Letters and logo elements are CNC-cut, routed, or cast — then finished, painted, or polished to your specs. Typical fabrication time is 2–3 weeks depending on material and complexity. We quality-check every piece before scheduling installation.

4
Step Four

Installation

Our install team mounts the sign — letters level, spacing consistent, anchored correctly for the wall type. For standoff mounts we pre-drill to studs or use appropriate wall anchors. For halo-lit signs we coordinate the low-voltage LED wiring. Install is typically completed in a half day. You don't need to vacate the office.
