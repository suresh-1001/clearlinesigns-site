About Outdoor Signs

What Are Outdoor Signs
and Why Do They Matter?

Outdoor signs are any signage installed on the exterior of your building, property, or grounds — visible to foot traffic and passing vehicles. They serve as your business's first impression and most persistent marketing asset. Unlike digital ads that stop when you stop paying, a well-built outdoor sign works around the clock for years.

In San Jose and the South Bay, exterior signage is also regulated. Most permanent signs require a sign permit from the city, and properties in shopping centers typically require landlord approval as well. Sign size, height, illumination, and placement are all governed by local codes — which vary by zoning district, building type, and city. We handle that entire process on your behalf.

Material selection matters too. California's UV exposure and coastal air are hard on signage. We spec aluminum cabinets, UV-stable vinyls, powder-coat finishes, and commercial-grade LED modules that maintain their appearance for 7–10+ years without fading or corroding.

Types of Outdoor Signs

Every Exterior Sign Type
We Design and Install

Storefront Signs

Face-mounted signs installed directly on your building facade. Available as flat panel, push-through acrylic, backlit cabinet, or dimensional lettering. The most common exterior sign for retail, restaurants, and professional services in San Jose.

Channel Letter Signs

Individual three-dimensional letters mounted to your building or a raceway. Front-lit, halo-lit, or non-illuminated. The standard for shopping center storefronts, national retailers, and businesses that want maximum night-time visibility.

Monument Signs

Freestanding ground-level signs at property entrances. Single-tenant or multi-tenant directory styles. Built in aluminum, stucco, stone veneer, or HDU — matched to your building's architecture and HOA requirements.

Cabinet Signs

Enclosed aluminum or acrylic box signs with an illuminated or non-lit face. Single-faced or double-faced. Versatile for high-visibility applications — works on building facades, poles, or monument bases.

Pylon & Pole Signs

Tall freestanding signs visible from the street or freeway. Used by shopping centers, hotels, gas stations, and multi-tenant properties that need high-elevation visibility from a distance. Requires structural engineering and city permits.

Building & Fascia Signs

Large-format signage spanning full building facades — common for corporate campuses, warehouses, and big-box retail. Dimensional letters, illuminated panels, or vinyl graphics applied directly to the building surface.

Blade & Projecting Signs

Signs that extend perpendicular from a building wall — also called hanging signs or projecting signs. Visible to pedestrians walking parallel to the building. Common in downtown San Jose and mixed-use retail corridors.

Window Graphics & Vinyl

Frosted, cut vinyl, perforated, or full-color printed graphics applied to storefront windows. Adds branding, privacy, or promotional messaging without permanent structure. No permit required in most cases.

Post & Panel Signs

Two-post signs with a flat panel face — used for property identification, real estate, construction sites, and businesses that need quick-turn or temporary exterior signage. Available in aluminum, ACM, and corrugated PVC.
