About Outdoor Signs

What Are Outdoor Signs
and Why Do They Matter?

Outdoor signs are any signage installed on the exterior of your building, property, or grounds — visible to foot traffic and passing vehicles. Business signs for outside serve as your company's first impression and most persistent marketing asset — outdoor business signs work around the clock for years, unlike digital ads that stop the moment you stop paying.

In San Jose and the South Bay, outdoor building signage and exterior signs are regulated. Most permanent signs require a sign permit from the city, and properties in shopping centers typically require landlord approval as well. Sign size, height, illumination, and placement are all governed by local codes — which vary by zoning district, building type, and city. We handle that entire process on your behalf, including site plans, elevation drawings, electrical calculations, and landlord approval packages.

Material selection matters too. California's UV exposure and coastal air are hard on signage. We spec aluminum cabinets, UV-stable vinyls, powder-coat finishes, and commercial-grade LED modules that maintain their appearance for 7–10+ years without fading or corroding.

Sign Types

Custom Outdoor & Exterior Signs
We Design & Install

Not every business needs the same sign. Here's what we offer and what each is best for.

Storefront Signs

Face-mounted signs installed directly on your building facade — flat panel, push-through acrylic, backlit cabinet, or dimensional lettering. The most common exterior sign type for retail and professional services in San Jose.

Retail · Restaurant · Office

Channel Letter Signs

Individual 3D letters mounted to your building or raceway — front-lit, halo-lit, or non-illuminated. The standard for shopping center storefronts and businesses that need maximum night-time visibility.

Illuminated · Night Visibility

Monument Signs

Freestanding ground-level signs at property entrances — single-tenant or multi-tenant directory styles. Built in aluminum, stucco, stone veneer, or HDU to match your building's architecture and HOA requirements.

Office Parks · Multi-Tenant

Cabinet Signs

Enclosed aluminum box signs with an illuminated or non-lit face. Single or double-faced. Versatile for high-visibility applications — works on building facades, poles, or monument bases.

Backlit · Double-Faced

Pylon & Pole Signs

Tall freestanding signs visible from the street or freeway — used by shopping centers, hotels, and multi-tenant properties that need high-elevation visibility. Requires structural engineering and city permits.

High Visibility · Freeway

Post & Panel Signs

Two-post signs with a flat panel face — used for property identification, real estate, construction sites, and businesses that need quick-turn exterior signage. Available in aluminum, ACM, and reflective sheeting.

Real Estate · Construction

Building & Fascia Signs

Large-format signage spanning full building facades — common for corporate campuses, warehouses, and big-box retail. Dimensional letters, illuminated panels, or vinyl graphics applied directly to the building surface.

Campus · Warehouse · Large Format

Blade & Projecting Signs

Signs that extend perpendicular from the building face — visible to foot traffic walking parallel to your storefront. Common in downtown San Jose and walkable retail corridors.

Downtown · Pedestrian Traffic

Window & Door Graphics

Frosted, cut vinyl, perforated, or full-color printed graphics applied to storefront windows and doors. Adds branding, privacy, or promotional messaging without permanent structure. No permit required in most cases.

No Permit · Fast Turnaround
