About Construction Signs

Construction Site Signage
That Works as Hard as Your Crew

A construction site is one of the most visible marketing opportunities a developer or general contractor has. For months — sometimes years — thousands of people drive or walk past your project every day. The right fence wrap, project identification sign, or "coming soon" display turns that exposure into awareness for your brand, your project, and your future tenants or buyers.

We've worked on active job sites across San Jose, including large-scale development projects downtown and along key corridors near the Diridon Station area. Construction signage has its own requirements: materials need to handle wind load and UV exposure for extended periods, grommets and mounting hardware need to be heavy-duty, and installation needs to work around active site access and safety zones. We understand how construction sites operate and coordinate accordingly.

Beyond branding, job sites in San Jose require specific safety and regulatory signs — OSHA-mandated postings, restricted area warnings, emergency contact displays, and contractor identification boards. We handle those alongside the marketing signage so you're getting everything from one vendor.

Sign Options

Types of Construction Signs
We Design & Install

Fence Wrap Banners

Full-span mesh or solid vinyl graphics printed and installed across chain-link or hoarding fences. Available in any length — from a single panel to hundreds of linear feet of site perimeter.

Project Identification Signs

Dibond or substrate-mounted project signs displaying the development name, architect, contractor, and contact information. Post-and-panel or wall-mounted formats available.

Coming Soon & Leasing Signs

"Coming soon," "now leasing," and "now pre-selling" displays for commercial and residential developments — sized for street visibility and built to last through the full construction period.

Hoarding & Barricade Graphics

Printed panels for temporary hoarding walls in urban construction zones. Turns plywood barricades into branded displays that communicate your project vision to pedestrians.

Safety & Regulatory Signs

OSHA-required postings, restricted area signs, hard hat and PPE requirement notices, emergency contact boards, and site entry compliance signage for GCs and subcontractors.

Wayfinding & Traffic Signs

Directional signs for site access, parking, staging areas, and pedestrian detours around active construction zones — built on aluminum or coroplast for easy repositioning as the site evolves.
